# Core Engraving Artwork Revision Design

## Objective

Replace the flat SVG cutaway proof with a text-free, image-generated architectural engraving while retaining deterministic brand typography and factual product structure.

## Visual Direction

The Core hero and card use a warm technical-paper ground with a refined navy architectural engraving. The environment shows a foundation cutaway through rooms, bridges, rails, portals, layered slabs, and machinery. Fine cross-hatching and restrained ink wash provide depth without becoming photorealistic.

The generated environment contains no readable text, logos, user interface, claims, badges, or watermarks.

## Brand Lockup

The title becomes one continuous `CAPELL CORE` lockup. `CAPELL` is the real vendored wordmark, including its curved A and underline. `CORE` follows on the same baseline in a light, high-contrast serif treatment matched to the wordmark. The former bold geometric sans-serif title is removed.

## Product Evidence

Full screenshots are removed from the campaign composition. Instead, deterministic pastel wireframe displays trace the recognizable structure of the real Core page-hierarchy and settings captures. The wireframes use simplified blocks, rows, navigation rails, controls, and hierarchy lines derived from those factual captures; they contain no invented labels, fake data, or generated UI text.

Amber, powder blue, soft emerald, and muted coral distinguish the display regions and journey signals. Navy framing keeps the wireframes integrated with the engraving.

## Composition

The hero retains the promise `The structure beneath every site`, the seven Core layers, and the four-stage journey Define → Connect → Resolve → Extend. The card remains independently composed with the Capell Core lockup, one dominant cutaway, simplified page-structure wireframe, and three compact stages.

The engraved environment supplies depth and atmosphere. The wordmark, `CORE`, promise, labels, journey markers, arrows, layer names, and pastel product wireframes remain deterministic overlays.

## Production and Validation

The renderer accepts a committed text-free generated environment as its base layer, then adds deterministic overlays and exports progressive sRGB JPEGs at 2880×960 and 800×500. The card is reviewed again at 400×250. Validation must confirm that generated pixels contain no text/UI/logo content and that the deterministic lockup, journey, and wireframe hierarchy remain legible.
